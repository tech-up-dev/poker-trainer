# C-bet Sizing on Dry Boards
- principle_tag: character_mapping
- concept: cbet_sizing_dry_boards
- difficulty: intermediate

## Q: On a K-7-2 rainbow flop as the preflop raiser versus one caller, what is the best c-bet size?
- [x] Small, 25-33% pot :: Dry boards favor the aggressor's range, so a small bet denies equity cheaply and keeps worse hands in.
- [ ] Large, 75% pot :: Overbetting a dry board bloats the pot with a range that does not need protection.
- [ ] Check :: Checking surrenders the range and initiative advantage on a board that smashes your range.
- [ ] All-in :: Wildly over-sized; it folds out everything you beat and only continues against better.
> glossary: c-bet, dry board

## Q: Villain is a Passive Loose Fish who calls too much. How should you adjust your c-bet plan?
- [x] Bet for thin value and rarely bluff :: Calling stations pay off wider than they fold, so thin value prints and bluffs burn money.
- [ ] Bluff more often :: Bluffing a player who does not fold is lighting money on fire.
- [ ] Check everything :: Checking gives up value you would otherwise collect from a wide calling range.
- [ ] Always overbet :: Overbetting without a value-heavy plan just builds a pot you are guessing in.
> glossary: thin value, calling station
